3.4 Fuel System
Gasoline from the fuel tank is pumped by the electrical fuel pump and passes through the fuel filter. After filtration of the fuel from filter, Fuel goes to the fuel metering valve which is a part of High pressure pump. Based on the position of fuel metering solenoid valve, Fuel enters the high pressure pump and gets pressurised. This high pressure fuel enters the Common rail , where the pressure is measured by the rail pressure sensor. Based on the signals from various sensors, EMS ECU controls the opening and closing time of the injector.
Canister operation
Evaporated fuel vapours from the fuel tank is guided towards the charcoal canister where they get adsorbed. Based on the requirement, EMS ECU actuates the purge solenoid valve and the adsorbed petrol vapours enters into the intake system.
