3.6 Cooling system
Coolant from the reservoir (Degassing tank) is sucked into the cooling system by the water pump. The suction created by the water pump also allows the coolant to enter the turbocharger and enters the water pump. The Coolant is now pumped into the cylinder block through the coolant outlet. After passing through the engine oil galleries, the hot coolant now enters the radiator and some part of hot coolant enters the heater core.
The coolant temperature is measured using the coolant temperature sensor and the signal is sent to EMS ECU. The coolant now passes through the radiator and gets cooled. EMS ECU sends signal to SPCM controller to switch ON the radiator fan based on Coolant temperature sensor signals. Now the coolant from radiator and heater core enters the Water pump. The coolant from heater core and turbocharger gets bypassed through the coolant bypass outlet to the cylinder block. Coolant from radiator reaches the thermostat valve inside the water pump module.
Thermostat valve is a coolant flow control device which is operated by virtue of heat received from the engine coolant flowing across it. Thermostat valve helps in regulating the operating temperature of engine by controlling the flow of coolant into the engine block. Once the temperature of the coolant reaches a certain limit, the thermostat valve opens and allows the coolant from the radiator to flow into the engine block. Thus the thermostat keeps the coolant temperature within the range to improve the combustion efficiency of the engine.
•  Thermostat start opening at 88 ± 2 ° C — 0.1 mm travel
•  Thermostat full opens at 96 ± 2 ° C — 8 mm travel
