21.6.2 WIRING REPAIR USING SELF-SOLDER SLEEVE

•  Wiring Repair of Airbag related wires & CAN wires are strictly prohibited
•  Wiring Harness Repair is not recommended for warranty jobs
Step 1: To join two wires, each 0.35 mm² together (1- to -1 type operation), Add cross section of two wires (0.35 + 0.35 = 0.7 mm2)
Step 2: As sum of cross section of wire is 0.7 mm2, choose white sleeve
Step 3: Cut the damaged wire. The cut must be made at a distance of > 50 mm from the damaged section and in an area where the sleeve will remain straight
Step 4: Choose Wire Strip length as 10 mm on both the wires. Strip the ends of the wires using stripping plier.
Step 5: Insert the self solder sleeve on one of the wire
Step 6: Connect the wires by twisting the stripped sections around each other.
Step 7: Slide the sleeve over the stripped section. The ring of solder should end up in the center of the stripped and twisted wires (A). The stripped and twisted section of the wires must be located between the 2 sealing rings (B)
•  When twisting the wires , make sure that your hands are not greasy so that the solder adheres
•  When the sleeve is positioned, check that all the copper strands remain flat and subsequently are not at risk of Puncturing the sleeve. Start the operation to join the wires again if necessary
•  The sealing rings must be clearly located on the insulated section of the wires(B) and not on the copper or where the copper and the insulated section meet. Start the operation to join the wires again if necessary.
Step 8: Check that the sensitive areas close to the sleeve and the other electrical wires are not at risk of damage. Protect sensitive areas close to the wiring with the heat shield
Step 9: Fit the required nozzle on the heat gun & turn it ON. Then Set Temperature in the heat gun as 300 °C.
Step 10: Heat the sleeve starting at the centre, concentrating the heat on the ring of solder and the stripped section of the wires.
•  Spend the majority of the recommended time on the ring solder and the rest of the time on the ends of the sleeve.
•  Heating operation takes 30 ± 10 seconds. This is a guide only. It can be reduced of increased depending on heating efficiency
Step 11: When the copper and the ring of solder become hot enough, the ring of solder melts and runs in between the copper strands (C).When the solder has completely melted, move the nozzle to the ends of the sleeve to finish shrinking the sheath.
•  Don’t overheat the sleeve
•  Don’t move the sleeve or wires until they have cooled
•  Don’t try to heat the sheath too quickly, use a backwards and forwards motion to ensure it does not burn.
•  Don’t direct the flow of hot air into the insulation of the wires so as not to burn it.
•  Wait minimum 1 minute for the sleeve to cool before deploying it to maintain the quality of the solder.
•  Don’t bend the sleeve (even when cool) so as to not damage the solder or the sealing properties of the sleeve.
21.6.3 WIRING REPAIR USING CRIMP SLEEVE
Crimping Sleeve (1 to 1)
Crimping Sleeve (2 to 1)
Step 1: To join three wires (2 to 1 operation), one side the 2 wires (A and B) both have a 0.5 mm2 section. On the other side wire C has a 1 mm² section. Add cross section of wires (0.5 + 0.5+1 = 2 mm2)
Step 2: As sum of cross section of three wires is 2 mm2, choose blue sleeve
Step 3: Cut the damaged wire. The cut must be made at a distance of > 50 mm from the damaged section and in an area where the sleeve will remain straight
Step 4: Choose Wire Strip length as 10 mm on both the wires. Strip the ends of the wires using stripping plier.
Step 5: Twist together the stripped section of the two wires with identical cross section (0.5 mm2). Then insert the crimp sleeve and join three wires are shown in figure (E)
Step 6: If the 2 wires have different diameters, twist the stripped section of the smaller wire around that of the larger wire before inserting them in the crimp sleeve as shown in figure (F)
•  When twisting the wires , make sure that your hands are not greasy so that the solder adheres
•  When the sleeve is positioned, check that all the copper strands remain flat and subsequently are not at risk of Puncturing the sleeve. Start the operation to join the wires again if necessary
Step 7: Crimping
1. Use crimping pliers and squeeze the handles fully together to unlock the pliers and open the jaws
2. On the pliers, find the crimping jaws which correspond to the sleeve chosen.
3. Insert the wire(s) into one end of the sleeve up to the stop located inside the metal barrel of the sleeve
4. Position the pliers' jaws around the middle of the section of the metal barrel where the wire(s) are
5. Squeeze the handles fully together to crimp the sleeve onto the wire(s). Crimping is complete once the pliers are opened again
Step 8: Repeat the crimping operation on the other end of the sleeve as shown in the figure. The pliers should have correctly crimped each wire on the halves of the metal barrel without damaging the plastic
•  Check that the copper of the wire is correctly crimped and that the plastic part of the sleeve has not been cut.
•  If the wire has been stripped by the correct length and correctly inserted in the metal barrel, then the copper of the wire should just extend from the shaft
Step 9: Check that the sensitive areas close to the sleeve and the other electrical wires are not at risk of damage. Protect sensitive areas close to the wiring with the heat shield
Step 10: Fit the required nozzle on the heat gun & turn it ON. Then Set Temperature in the heat gun as 300 °C. When the sheath starts to contract, move the nozzle to one of the ends
•  Spend the majority of the recommended time on the ring solder and the rest of the time on the ends of the sleeve.
•  Heating operation takes 30 ± 10 seconds. This is a guide only. It can be reduced of increased depending on heating efficiency
Step 11: The sheath continues to contract and produces a glue. When the end of the sleeve has completely contracted and the glue has correctly covered the opening of the sleeve, repeat the operation on the other end to obtain the result as shown in the figure
Step 12: When the copper and the ring of solder become hot enough, the ring of solder melts and runs in between the copper strands (C).When the solder has completely melted, move the nozzle to the ends of the sleeve to finish shrinking the sheath.
•  Don’t try to heat the sheath too quickly, use a backwards and forwards motion to ensure it does not burn.
•  Don’t direct the flow of hot air into the insulation of the wires so as not to burn it.
•  Wait minimum 1 minute for the sleeve to cool before deploying it to maintain the quality of the solder.
•  Don’t bend the sleeve (even when cool) so as to not damage the solder or the sealing properties of the sleeve.
21.6.4 VISUAL CHECK
Correctly applied self-solder sleeve
Once wiring repair is done, the following points need to be checked
•  The insulation of the wires is within the sleeve
•  (Self-solder sleeves) The sealing rings must be clearly located on the insulated section of the wires.
•  (Self-solder sleeves) The solder has completely melted.
•  (Crimp sleeves) The adhesive of the crimp sleeves forms a barrier at the ends of the sleeves
•  The sleeves has completely contracted around the insulation of the wires
•  The sleeve is not cut, slit, discolored or pierced by a copper strand.
•  The insulation of the wires does not show any sign of damage caused by overheating.
•  The wiring and the area surrounding it have not been damaged by the heating operation.
•  After any operation on a wiring harness, check and ensure that no electrical DTC’s found in the diagnostic tool
•  Check that the component or the function of the repaired line is working correctly, carry out a test drive.
•  If a DTC code is present, refer diagnostic procedure given in the respective diagnostic manual
